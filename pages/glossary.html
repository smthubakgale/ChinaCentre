A

  • Accent chair: A chair used to add a decorative touch to a room.
  • Accent table: A small table used to add a decorative touch to a room.
  • Armchair: A chair with arms, designed for comfort and relaxation.
  • Armoire: A large, ornate cabinet used for storing clothing and other personal items.

B

  • Banquette: A built-in bench, often used in dining areas.
  • Baseboard: A type of molding used to cover the joint between a wall and floor.
  • Bench: A long, low seat, often used in entryways or mudrooms.
  • Bookcase: A piece of furniture used for storing books and other personal items.

C

  • Console table: A table placed behind a sofa or in a hallway.
  • Coffee table: A low table placed in front of a sofa or armchair.
  • Corner desk: A desk designed to fit into a corner, often used in home offices.
  • Chest of drawers: A piece of furniture used for storing clothing and other personal items.

D

  • Dining table: A table used for eating meals
  • Desk lamp: A lamp designed to be placed on a desk or table.
  • Dresser: A piece of furniture used for storing clothing and other personal items.

E

  • End table: A small table placed next to a sofa or armchair.
  • Executive desk: A large, formal desk designed for use in an office.

F

  • Futon: A piece of furniture that can be used as both a sofa and a bed.
  • Frame: The structural part of a piece of furniture that provides support and shape.

G

  • Glaze: A type of finish applied to furniture to give it a glossy appearance.
  • Guest bed: A bed designed for use by guests, often smaller than a standard bed.

H

  • Headboard: A piece of furniture attached to the head of a bed, often decorative.
  • Hideaway bed: A bed that can be folded up and hidden away when not in use.

I

  • Inlay: A decorative technique where small pieces of material are inserted into a larger piece.
  • Ivory: A type of material often used in furniture making, particularly for decorative elements.

J

  • Jacquard: A type of fabric often used in upholstery.

K

  • Kitchen cart: A piece of furniture designed to provide additional storage and counter space in a kitchen.

L

  • Ladder back chair: A type of chair characterized by a ladder-like design on the back.
  • Lamp table: A small table designed to hold a lamp.

M

  • Mahogany: A type of wood often used in furniture making.
  • Mattress: A cushioned pad designed to be used on a bed.

N

  • Nightstand: A small table designed to be placed next to a bed.

O

  • Oak: A type of wood often used in furniture making.
  • Ottoman: A piece of furniture designed to provide additional seating or storage.

P

  • Pillow top mattress: A type of mattress characterized by a layer of padding on top.
  • Platform bed: A type of bed characterized by a solid, flat surface.

Q

  • Queen-sized bed: A type of bed characterized by a specific size (typically 60 inches wide and 80 inches long).

R

  • Recliner: A type of chair designed to recline backwards.
  • Rustic: A style of furniture characterized by a rough, natural look.

S

  • Sectional sofa: A type of sofa characterized by multiple sections that can
  • Solid wood: A type of wood that is made from a single piece of wood, rather than being engineered from multiple pieces.

T

  • Twin-sized bed: A type of bed characterized by a specific size (typically 39 inches wide and 75 inches long).
  • Table lamp: A type of lamp that is designed to be placed on a table.

U

  • Upholstery: The process of covering furniture with fabric or leather.

V

  • Veneer: A thin layer of wood that is applied to a surface to give it a decorative finish.

W

  • Wardrobe: A piece of furniture used for storing clothing and other personal items.
  • Wood finish: A type of finish applied to wood to give it a decorative appearance.

X

  • X-frame chair: A type of chair with an X-shaped frame.

Y

  • Yardage: A unit of measurement for fabric, equal to 36 inches.

Z

  • Zebra wood: A type of wood characterized by its striped pattern.
